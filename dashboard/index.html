    About this data
    Dataset

    This tool visualises structured data extracted from Biological Weapons Convention (BWC) Confidence-Building Measure (CBM) submissions. The dataset covers 510 publicly available submissions from 51 states parties, downloaded from the UN BWC portal (bwc-cbm.un.org), spanning 1988–2026.

    Restricted submissions purple on map

    China, France, Russia, and India have submitted CBMs to the Implementation Support Unit (ISU) but these are designated as confidential and are not publicly available. They appear shaded on the map. Their absence from the facility data does not reflect non-participation — it reflects a transparency choice or designation.

    Data extraction

    All structured information (facility names, locations, containment levels, organisational details) was extracted from PDF documents by a large language model (Anthropic Claude Sonnet). Extraction confidence scores (0–1) are shown per facility where available. Errors and omissions are possible, particularly for scanned or non-English submissions.

    Geocoding

    Facility locations are geocoded using OpenStreetMap Nominatim from the address and city fields declared in each submission. Match confidence is rated:

    • High (Nominatim importance > 0.6) — specific building or address match
    • Medium (0.3–0.6) — neighbourhood or district match
    • Low (< 0.3) — city-level or country-level only; may be inaccurate by tens of kilometres

    Facilities without a geocodable address or city are not shown on the map (~3% of records). Use the filter panel to hide low-confidence geocodes.

    Layers
    • Research facilities (Form A1) — declared biological research centres and laboratories, coloured by highest biosafety level (BSL)
    • Defence facilities (Form A Part 2) — facilities supporting declared national biological defence programmes
    • Vaccine facilities (Form G) — declared vaccine production facilities
    "Nothing to declare"

    In the compliance grid, orange cells indicate a country submitted that form but declared no relevant activity — this is substantively different from not submitting the form at all (dark cells). A country with all orange cells is actively participating but declaring no facilities.

    Form A1 submission rate (choropleth)

    The map background shows the proportion of a country's submissions that included substantive Form A1 data. A rate of 100% means every submission contained facility declarations. Some countries submit annually; others only occasionally.

    Data extracted and processed as part of academic research on BWC transparency. Not an official UN or ISU product. All source documents are publicly available at bwc-cbm.un.org.
